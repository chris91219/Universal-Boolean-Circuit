Relaxation-to-Circuit Gap: Theory Notes for NeurIPS Resubmission
=================================================================

Purpose
-------

The current experiments reveal that the differentiable UBC relaxation can solve many truth tables while the locally argmax-decoded symbolic circuit can fail. This is not just an implementation detail; it is a mathematically meaningful gap between:

1. the relaxed predictor evaluated by the soft forward pass; and
2. the deterministic discrete circuit obtained by local argmax decoding of every routing/gate/readout categorical distribution.

We should name and measure this gap explicitly, then connect it to theory and new rounding/hardening algorithms.


1. A Measurable Relaxation-to-Circuit Gap
-----------------------------------------

For each benchmark instance f on the full Boolean cube X = {0,1}^B, let:

soft(theta)(x)
    be the differentiable UBC forward pass.

hard(theta)(x)
    be the circuit obtained by local argmax decoding of all categorical selectors/gates.

Let y_f be the truth table of f. Define:

SoftEM(theta, f) =
    1{ threshold_0.5(soft(theta)(X)) = y_f }.

DecodedEM(theta, f) =
    1{ hard(theta)(X) = y_f }.

RelaxationGap(theta, f) =
    SoftEM(theta, f) - DecodedEM(theta, f).

At the run level:

RelaxationGapRate =
    mean_f SoftEM(theta_f, f) - mean_f DecodedEM(theta_f, f).

We can also use row-level versions:

SoftRowAcc(theta, f) =
    (1 / 2^B) sum_x 1{ threshold_0.5(soft(theta)(x)) = f(x) }.

DecodedRowAcc(theta, f) =
    (1 / 2^B) sum_x 1{ hard(theta)(x) = f(x) }.

RowRelaxationGap =
    SoftRowAcc - DecodedRowAcc.

This makes "measurable" precise: it is directly observable from the same truth-table evaluation protocol. The current numbers suggest a large gap: high soft EM can coexist with low decoded EM.


2. Why Local Argmax Can Fail
----------------------------

The UBC relaxation is a composition of categorical mixtures. Even when every local categorical has a high-probability option, the forward pass is not generally equal to the circuit obtained by independently taking local argmax choices.

Three mechanisms can create the gap:

1. Convex averaging of circuits.
   The soft model can combine several partially correct subcircuits and become correct after thresholding, even when none of the locally decoded paths is globally correct.

2. Local argmax is not MAP decoding.
   Choosing the top option independently at each selector is not the same as choosing the globally best discrete circuit under truth-table loss. Correlations across layers matter.

3. Early soft success can stop hardening.
   If early stopping monitors only soft EM, optimization may stop before categorical margins are large enough to guarantee stable decoding.

The experiments should therefore report both soft EM and decoded EM, plus entropy/margin diagnostics for all categorical choices.


3. Rounding Theorem Target
--------------------------

Goal:
    Prove a sufficient condition under which argmax decoding preserves correctness.

Definitions:

For each categorical distribution p over a gate/route/readout choice, define the categorical tail mass

tail(p) = 1 - max_i p_i.

Define the categorical margin

margin(p) = p_(1) - p_(2),

where p_(1) and p_(2) are the largest and second-largest entries.

For a trained soft circuit theta, define the output margin on the truth table:

gamma_out(theta, f) =
    min_{x in X} | soft(theta)(x) - 1/2 |.

Theorem Sketch A: Local Rounding Under Margins
----------------------------------------------

Let C_theta be a depth-Delta UBC soft circuit with bounded fan-in and bounded Lipschitz constants for each relaxed gate module on [0,1]^2. Let C_round be the circuit obtained by local argmax decoding.

Assume:

1. every gate/route/readout categorical distribution has tail mass at most epsilon_l at layer l;
2. the accumulated perturbation bound satisfies

       E_round <= Sum_{l=1}^Delta L_{l:Delta} epsilon_l < gamma_out(theta, f);

3. threshold_0.5(C_theta(X)) = y_f on the full truth table.

Then:

       C_round(X) = y_f.

Interpretation:

If the soft model is correct with positive output margin, and all categorical distributions are sufficiently sharp, local argmax decoding preserves exact match.

What this gives us:

1. A clean theorem connecting optimization diagnostics to decoded EM.
2. A reason to add margin/entropy regularization and a post-soft-success hardening phase.
3. A falsifiable empirical prediction: decoded failures should concentrate at low categorical margin or low output margin.


4. Existence/Rounding Theorem Target
------------------------------------

The local argmax theorem is sufficient but may be too strong. A softer and more general theorem is available if we interpret the trained UBC as a distribution over circuits.

Let Pi_theta be the distribution over discrete circuits induced by sampling all categorical gate/route/readout choices. Let loss(C, f) be truth-table 0-1 row loss:

loss(C, f) =
    (1 / 2^B) sum_x 1{ C(x) != f(x) }.

Theorem Sketch B: Low Expected Loss Implies a Good Discrete Circuit
-------------------------------------------------------------------

If

    E_{C ~ Pi_theta} loss(C, f) <= epsilon,

then there exists a discrete circuit C* in the support of Pi_theta such that

    loss(C*, f) <= epsilon.

Moreover, if we sample M independent circuits C_1,...,C_M from Pi_theta and score them on the full truth table, then

    P[min_m loss(C_m, f) <= epsilon / delta] >= 1 - delta^M

by Markov plus independence.

Interpretation:

Even when local argmax fails, the learned distribution may contain good discrete circuits. This motivates sampled decoding, beam search, or coordinate descent over discrete choices scored by truth-table loss.

What this gives us:

1. A theorem aligned with the original universality story, since the paper already models distributions over Boolean circuits.
2. A principled alternative to naive local argmax.
3. A direct empirical algorithm: sample or beam-decode circuits, score on the truth table, and report best decoded EM.


5. Optimization/Learnability Direction
--------------------------------------

A possible learnability result can be stated under a realizability-plus-margin assumption.

Assumptions:

1. The target f is represented by some circuit C* in the UBC architecture with depth Delta and width Upsilon.
2. The selector/gate logits have a neighborhood around C* in which C* remains the unique argmax with margin alpha.
3. The soft forward pass has output margin gamma on the truth table in that neighborhood.
4. The training objective includes BCE plus categorical entropy/margin regularization and an annealing schedule.

Possible result:

In the basin where all categorical margins exceed alpha/2 and output margin exceeds gamma/2, gradient steps that reduce the regularized loss preserve the decoded circuit and drive the soft predictor toward f. A final hardening phase increases categorical margins without changing the decoded function.

This is more local than global learnability, but it is realistic and directly tied to the implementation.


6. Experimental Plan Matching The Theory
----------------------------------------

The empirical section should include:

1. Soft EM, decoded EM, row-level soft/decoded accuracy, and RelaxationGapRate.
2. Categorical entropy and margin for gates, row selectors, and pair selectors.
3. Output margin min/mean over the truth table.
4. Early-stop step and whether early stopping triggered on decoded EM.
5. Controlled ablation of S_add, lifting, Tmin, entropy strength, and relaxation mode.
6. Local argmax vs sampled/beam/coordinate decoding.

The key plot:

    decoded EM versus categorical margin / output margin.

If the rounding theorem is right, decoded failures should occur mostly when the theorem's sufficient margin condition is violated.


7. Positioning
--------------

If universality remains the main theorem:

The paper can say: the architecture is intrinsically circuit-valued at the sampled/discrete level and universally expressive; the differentiable training relaxation is effective but introduces a measurable relaxation-to-circuit gap. We then quantify and reduce this gap.

If we add optimization theory:

The paper can say: beyond universality, we provide sufficient margin conditions for faithful rounding and an existence theorem justifying sampled/beam decoding. This addresses the reviewers' learnability concern without overclaiming global optimization guarantees.

Fallback story:

Even if decoded EM does not exceed MLP EM, the contribution can still be strong if we show:

1. high soft EM;
2. low BNR / low primitive faithfulness for matched MLPs;
3. a mathematically characterized relaxation-to-circuit gap;
4. a rounding/search method that improves decoded EM;
5. diagnostics that match the theorem's predicted failure modes.
